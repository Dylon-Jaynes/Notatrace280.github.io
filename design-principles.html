Visual Hierarchy:

There are many important elements on a page. Visual Hierarchy would suggest that we should emphasize from the most important elements to the least important elements. Consider the above image and how it uses a large image of their product to catch the interest of users and then proceeds to inform the users about their restaurant in the text below. Please visit Zevendesign.com for more information.

Hick's Law:

Hick's Law refers to how the amount of content on a webpage can heavily influence the users experience. This law suggests that we should limit the content we use on a webpage so as to not bog down the user with too much content. In the image above, notice how Slack uses simple descriptions to get the point across without using too many words. Visit Interaction-Design.org for more information about Hick's Law.

White Space and Clean Design:

What does it mean to have White Space and Clean Design on your webpage? White Space and Clean Web Design refers to the spacing in between elements on a page as well as the simplicity of the color and style of those elements. Look at the image above and notice the spacing between elements as well as the colors being used. Visit Webfx.com for more information.
